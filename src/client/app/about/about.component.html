Air Quality Radar

Air Quality Radar is a forecasting service for air pollution in and around Cambridge. It’s a weather forecast except instead of predicted sunshine and rainfall, it tells you the different levels of pollution to expect in the future.

How to use

The map shows a bird’s eye view of Cambridge. You can click-and-drag your way around the city and zoom in and out by scrolling up and down on your mouse. The red-tinted overlay shows the severity of the pollution at that point on the map. The redder a point is, the worse the pollution levels are.

You can find a specific area in Cambridge by typing it into the search bar above the map and selecting the location from the list that appears below. The map will show you the place you’re looking for.

If you want to change the time you’re looking at, the slider below the map can be clicked and dragged left or right to move the map backwards or forwards in time.

The tables and graphs to the right side of the map show the levels of different pollutants over time, averaged across the whole of Cambridge. These give you a general idea of the pollution trends for the whole city.

Meet the pollutants

  • PM2.5: These are tiny particles in the air with a diameter of less than 2.5 micrometres. Being very small, they can cause serious health problems by penetrating deep into lungs and the bloodstream. In high concentrations, they have been found to contribute to lung cancer and heart attacks.
  • PM10: These are small particles with a diameter of less than 10 micrometres. They carry the same risks associated with PM2.5, but as they are larger they have been found to be significantly less harmful to the body.
  • NOx: This is a catch-all label for Nitrogen Oxides. In extreme circumstances, it is responsible for smog and acid rain. It is created by sources combustion, such as road traffic. Long-term exposure has been linked to respiratory health problems including asthma.

Data sources

This application only uses open data sources. The current data sources for air quality information are:

  • Regent Street, provided by DEFRA
  • Parker Street, provided by Cambridge City Council
  • Gonville Place, provided by Cambridge City Council
  • Newmarket Road, provided by Cambridge City Council
  • Montague Road, provided by Cambridge City Council

This application also uses weather forecasts from the Met Office, and historical weather data from the University of Cambridge Computer Lab.

About the creators

Air Quality Radar is an open-source project created by students at the University of Cambridge in collaboration with Microsoft. The source code for the project is available on GitHub, and we are accepting pull requests if you'd like to improve the project in whatever way.
